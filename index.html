FAQ

The questions we get twice a day.

Still have one? hello@navly.co.uk — we reply the same day.
Do I need to tell my instructor?

Legally, yes — you should let your instructor know you're recording the lesson. In practice, 94% of UK instructors are fine with it once they see the readiness score. Navly is a coaching tool, not a scorecard for them.

If your instructor isn't happy, Navly has a "coaching-notes only" mode — audio is processed on-device, transcribed, and deleted within 60 seconds. Only your notes remain.

Is my audio private?

Yes. All transcription happens on your phone by default — nothing leaves the device. If you opt in to cloud sync for multi-device access, audio is encrypted in transit and deleted from our servers within 30 days. We never sell, share, or train on your recordings.

Full detail in our privacy policy.

How accurate is the readiness score?

In our UK cohort of 1,200 learners, 84% of people who booked at 85% readiness passed first time — vs. the national 49%. The score isn't magic, it just counts honestly. It can't make you a better driver, but it stops you booking a test you'd fail.

Does it work with automatic + manual?

Yes. Navly picks up your gearbox from the first lesson and adjusts the fault tracker — clutch control is hidden on automatic, for example. Everything else works identically.

What's the "pass-first guarantee"?

If you're on the 6-month plan, reach 85% readiness, and don't pass first time — we'll refund your plan fee and give you a free month while you prep for the resit. Only on the 6-month plan, and only when you've logged at least 15 hours of lessons in the app.

My instructor already gives me notes. Why Navly?

Because written notes are a summary, not a transcript. Your instructor writes "watch mirrors" once. Navly catches the 12 separate times they said it — and shows you which junction type triggers it.

Think of Navly as the thing that remembers what your instructor was too busy driving to write down.

Can I use Navly for my theory test?

Not yet. Navly is focused on the practical test — the bit most people fail. Theory prep is well-served by other apps; we'd rather do one thing right than two things half-right.

Is it a subscription? Can I pause?

Monthly is a subscription — cancel in one tap, inside the app. The 6-month plan is a single £38.99 payment with no auto-renew unless you turn it on. Both plans start with a free first lesson — no credit card required to try. You can also pause for up to 3 months if you're taking a break from lessons.
